Effects of air pollution

Climate change

Air pollution is the greatest environmental risk to health of our time, and climate change is the greatest public health threat of the 21st century.


Noncommunicable diseases

Air pollution is the second leading cause of noncommunicable diseases, such as stroke, cancer and heart disease, which are on the rise worldwide. [Source: WHO]

What should we do?

We all live under the same sky. Measures can be taken to protect us from air pollution. 1. Check the AQI on a daily basis if you live in serious polluted area. Avoid exposuring in the air for a long time. Try to wear mask when you are outdoor. 2. Try to reduce driving private cars and take public transportation instead.
